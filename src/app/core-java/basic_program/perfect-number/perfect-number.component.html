Perfect Number

Perfect number is a positive integer that is equal to the sum of its factors except itself.

For example:
The factors of 6 is 1*6 = 6 2*3 = 6 3*2 = 6
The factors of 6 is 1,2 and 3 and the sum of these factors is 1+2+3 is 6 which is equal to its original number.
So, 6 is a perfect number.
The factor of 10 is 1*10 = 10 2*5 = 10 5*2 = 10
The factors of 10 is 1,2 and 5 and the sum of these factors is 1+2+5 is 8 which is not equal to its original number.
So, 10 is not a perfect number.

Example - 1
Input

Enter a number

6

Output

Given number 6 is a perfect number

Example - 2
Input

Enter a number

10

Output

Given number 10 is not a perfect number

Example - 3
Input

Enter a number

28

Output

Given number 28 is a perfect number
